Types of Cyber Attacks

Cybercrime takes many forms, and it is essential to understand the different types of cyber attacks in order to protect yourself online. Below are some common types of cyberattacks, with real-time examples of how they are being used today.

Phishing

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals attempt to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers by pretending to be a trusted entity.

**Real-Time Example**: A recent example of phishing is the widespread attack on Microsoft users, where hackers impersonated Microsoft support and sent emails that appeared legitimate. Users were tricked into entering their credentials, which were then stolen.

Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's data, making it inaccessible until a ransom is paid to the attacker.

**Real-Time Example**: The 2021 attack on the Colonial Pipeline was a significant ransomware attack, which disrupted the U.S. fuel supply. The hackers demanded a ransom in exchange for the decryption key.

DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)

DDoS attacks occur when a large number of compromised computers are used to flood a server with traffic, making the server slow or inaccessible.

**Real-Time Example**: In 2020, Amazon Web Services (AWS) suffered one of the largest-ever DDoS attacks, which involved a record-breaking 2.3 terabits per second of traffic. This attack caused significant slowdowns for some of AWS's clients.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attack

MitM attacks involve an attacker intercepting and potentially altering communication between two parties without their knowledge.

**Real-Time Example**: A recent attack on a popular online banking app used a MitM technique to intercept transactions and steal money by injecting fake pages requesting additional information from users.

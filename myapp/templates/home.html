What is Cyber Crime?

Cybercrime is any criminal activity that involves a computer, networked device, or a network. It includes a wide range of offenses, from hacking, identity theft, online fraud, to cyberbullying. As the digital world grows, so does the risk of cybercrime. Criminals use the internet to carry out malicious activities for financial gain, espionage, or to cause harm.

Cybercrime can affect individuals, businesses, and governments. Common examples of cybercrime include: hacking into systems, data breaches, phishing scams, and spreading viruses or malware. To combat cybercrime, various law enforcement agencies, along with international cooperation, have made efforts to fight against cyber threats. Awareness, digital literacy, and security measures like strong passwords, encryption, and two-factor authentication can help prevent cybercrimes.
